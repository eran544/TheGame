# The Game — Rulebook

**Designer:** Steffen Benndorf  
**Players:** 1–5  
**Age:** 8+  
**Duration:** Approx. 20 minutes  

---

## Overview

In *The Game*, everyone plays together as a team trying to lay as many number cards as possible—ideally all 98—into four rows in the middle of the table.  

There are:

- **4 Row Cards**
  - 2 Ascending Rows (from 1 → 99)
  - 2 Descending Rows (from 100 → 2)
- **98 Number Cards** (numbered 2–99)

## The Rows of Cards: Rules for Laying Cards

- In an **ascending** row, each new card must be **greater** than the card before it.
  - Example sequences: `3, 11, 12, 13, 18, 20, 34, 35, 51` or `2, 7, 19, 25, 28, 49`.

- In a **descending** row, each new card must be **smaller** than the one before.
  - Example sequences: `94, 90, 78, 61, 60, 57` or `98, 97, 88, 83, 81`.

📝 **Tip:** The smaller the gaps between cards, the better — this allows more cards to be played.

🃏 **Note:** Stack the number cards on top of each other (not side by side). Only the top card of each pile should be visible.

## Getting Started

1. **Setup**
   - Place the **four row cards** face up in the center of the table (two ascending, two descending).
   - Shuffle the **98 number cards** thoroughly.
   - Deal cards as follows:
     - 1-player: 8 cards  
     - 2-player: 7 cards each  
     - 3–5 players: 6 cards each  
   - Form a **draw pile** with the remaining cards.

2. **Example Setup Illustration**
   - *(Depiction shows four row cards arranged vertically on the table with a face-down draw pile beside them and hands of cards in front of players.)*

## How to Play

1. **Turn Order**
   - Decide together who goes first, then play clockwise.
   - On your turn, you must play **at least two cards** from your hand onto any piles (one or more).
   - You may play more if possible — even all your cards.

2. **Playing Cards**
   - Choose any pile and lay cards **one by one**.
   - Follow ascending/descending order rules for each respective pile.
   - You may place multiple cards on the same or different piles in any sequence.

### Example Turn
Linus’s turn:
- He lays 1 card on the first ascending pile.
- Then 2 cards on the second ascending pile.
- Then 1 card on the last descending pile.

After playing, he draws the same number of cards from the draw pile to refill his hand.

---

## The Backwards Trick

As piles grow, you can use a special move called the **Backwards Trick** to reverse order temporarily.

- On an **ascending pile**, you may play a card that is **exactly 10 less** than the top card.
- On a **descending pile**, you may play a card that is **exactly 10 greater** than the top card.

You may use this trick **as often as you like** and on any piles during your turn.

### Examples

- **Ascending Pile Example:**
  - The top card is `47`.
  - Normally you’d play a card higher than 47.
  - Tim plays `37` (exactly 10 less), so it’s allowed.

- **Descending Pile Example:**
  - The top card is `65`.
  - Normally you’d play a card lower than 65.
  - Sarah plays `75` (exactly 10 greater), so it’s allowed.

## Permissible Communication

Players **cannot** reveal or ask about exact numbers on their cards.

❌ Forbidden:
- Asking “Do you have 42?”  
- Saying “I can play 53.”

✅ Allowed:
- “Don’t lay on the last pile.”  
- “Try not to make a big jump here.”

This limited communication adds cooperative tension and strategy.

---

## End of Game

1. **When the Draw Pile is Empty:**
   - Continue playing without drawing new cards.
   - From this point, each player only needs to play **one** card per turn.

2. **Game Over:**
   - The game ends when a player cannot play the minimum required number of cards:
     - 2 cards (if draw pile remains)
     - 1 card (if draw pile is empty)

3. **Scoring:**
   - Count all unplayed cards:
     - Remaining in players’ hands **+** any left in the draw pile.
   - The fewer remaining cards, the better.

| Result | Description |
|:--|:--|
| 0 cards left | Perfect game – you beat *The Game*! |
| < 10 cards left | Excellent result |
| ≥ 10 cards left | Try again for a better score |

---

## Expert Variant

For a greater challenge:

- Each player must play **at least 3 cards** per turn.
- Reduce the number of cards dealt:
  - 3–5 players: 5 cards  
  - 2 players: 6 cards  
  - 1 player: 7 cards  

Everything else remains the same.
